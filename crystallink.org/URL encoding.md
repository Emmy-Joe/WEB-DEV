Here is a list of commonly used URL encoding for various characters:

- Space: `%20`
- Exclamation mark (`!`): `%21`
- Double quote (`"`): `%22`
- Hash (`#`): `%23`
- Dollar sign (`$`): `%24`
- Percent (`%`): `%25`
- Ampersand (`&`): `%26`
- Single quote (`'`): `%27`
- Open parenthesis (`(`): `%28`
- Close parenthesis (`)`): `%29`
- Asterisk (`*`): `%2A`
- Plus (`+`): `%2B`
- Comma (`,`): `%2C`
- Hyphen (`-`): `%2D`
- Period (`.`): `%2E`
- Slash (`/`): `%2F`
- Colon (`:`): `%3A`
- Semicolon (`;`): `%3B`
- Less than (`<`): `%3C`
- Equal (`=`): `%3D`
- Greater than (`>`): `%3E`
- Question mark (`?`): `%3F`
- At sign (`@`): `%40`
- Open bracket (`[`): `%5B`
- Backslash (`\`): `%5C`
- Close bracket (`]`): `%5D`
- Caret (`^`): `%5E`
- Underscore (`_`): `%5F`
- Grave accent/backtick (`\``): `%60`
- Open brace (`{`): `%7B`
- Vertical bar/pipe (`|`): `%7C`
- Close brace (`}`): `%7D`
- Tilde (`~`): `%7E`

For example, if you want to encode the message "Hello, World!", you would replace spaces with `%20` and the comma with `%2C`, resulting in "Hello%2C%20World%21".
